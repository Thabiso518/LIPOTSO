11. Sample Answer:

Traditional communication refers to analog, one-way methods like face-to-face meetings, print media, and broadcast media. Digital communication uses internet-based tools like mobile phones, social media, and email. Key differences include digital being faster, more interactive, having greater reach, and being more cost-effective at scale.

Section C: Essay Questions

16. Sample Answer:

Mobile phones and laptops play complementary roles in ICT4D. Mobile phones, particularly basic feature phones, are the "tool of reach and access" - they are ubiquitous, affordable, and portable, making them ideal for reaching the "last mile." They enable communication, mobile money, SMS alerts, and simple data collection.

Laptops serve as the "tool of depth and creation" - they are used for data analysis, content creation, software development, and program management. While mobile phones collect data from the field, laptops analyze this data and transform it into actionable insights.

The symbiotic relationship between these devices is crucial: data flows from phones to laptops, and insights flow from laptops back to phones for implementation.

17. Sample Answer:

Technology can contribute to development by improving access to services, increasing efficiency, and enabling innovation. For example, mobile money has revolutionized financial inclusion in Kenya through M-PESA, allowing people without bank accounts to save and transfer money.

However, technology can also worsen inequality through the digital divide. Those without access to devices, electricity, or digital skills are left further behind. For instance, during the COVID-19 pandemic, students without internet access couldn't participate in online learning, exacerbating educational inequalities.

The impact depends on implementation: technology must be context-appropriate, affordable, and accompanied by digital literacy training to avoid worsening existing inequalities.
